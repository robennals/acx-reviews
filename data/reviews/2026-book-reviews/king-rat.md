I am not sure if there was a real King. I am not sure there was a Grey.

I believe there was a Sean. I think her invention is beyond Clavell’s power.

She does nothing for the plot.  


The most jarring thing to a reader in 2026, is that this 1962 had a transgender woman character, treated with great sympathy with the omniscient narrator (and nearly everyone else in the camp) using her chosen pronouns. Such sympathy reads, to me, like atonement.

Sean is not necessary to the plot of King Rat. She does nothing to advance the diamond ring scheme. She does not affect the power struggle between the King and Grey. She exists in the novel as a presence, not a function, and this is the first indication that something different is happening with her.

**Rat kings** form in confined spaces — burrows, hay bales, ship rafters — when young rats huddle together for warmth and their tails get fixed by sebum, sap, frost, or blood. Then they struggle to free themselves and the struggling tightens the knot. The harder they pull, the more bound they become.
